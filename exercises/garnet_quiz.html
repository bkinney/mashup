    2. Under plane polarized light, the mineral appears blue.

  2. Under plane polarized light, slowly rotate the stage 180°. What do you observe?
    1. As I rotate the stage counter-clockwise, the image rotates clockwise. Apart from that, not much happens.
      1. True. Light microscopes behave that way because images are inverted by the magnifying lenses.
    2. As I rotate the stage, the colors in the mineral change.
      1. Please look again. Remember,plane polarization is active when the switch is positioned on the left.
  3. Now switch to cross polarized light, and rotate the stage again. What do you observe?
    1. The brightness of some areas changes as the specimen rotates.
      1. correct
    2. Same as before, except bluer.
      1. Try again. Focus on the brightest areas of the specimen.
